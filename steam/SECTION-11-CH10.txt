[h1]Part 3: Automation & Empire[/h1]

[h2]Chapter 10: Grey Labyrinth Elite Asset Acquisition[/h2]

[b]Primary Objective:[/b] Enter the Grey Labyrinth, capture elite slimes (Twin, Sloomber, Hyper), and learn the hazards before they learn you.

[b]Mindset:[/b] This is an extraction run, not a homestead. You go in, grab the high-value stock, and leave. The Labyrinth punishes greed and rewards a clean exit.

[h3]Prerequisites & Entry[/h3]

[b]Required Equipment:[/b] Heart Module III (250 HP), Power Core III (250 Energy), Jetpack II, Water Tank, Pulse Wave, Tank Guard

[b]Entry Tool - Radiant Projector:[/b]
[list]
[*]Blueprint from Viktor Humphries (Day 20+ after popping Boom or Flutter Gordo)
[*]Craft 3-4 Radiant Projectors (500 NB each + 10 Hunter Plorts + 10 Boom Plorts + 5 Silky Sand + 5 Lava Dust)
[*]Powers the light puzzles that unlock the main entrance
[/list]

[b]Entry Location:[/b] Starlight Strand, through the door directly under where the Flutter Gordo sat (RECOMMENDED route)

Inside, Radiant Slimes (rare prismatic variants of any species) shed Prisma energy while keeping their radiant colors. Expect themed decoratable rooms with alcoves for favored slimes, a "Lost and Found" that recovers Radiant Slimes lost in base form, and a new currency, [b]Sprinkles[/b], spent on Sanctuary expansions, themes, gadgets, and decor. See SECTION-01 and SECTION-17 for full detail.

[h3]Phase 1: Understanding Grey Labyrinth Mechanics[/h3]

[b]Critical Hazards:[/b]
[list]
[*][b]Prisma Disruptions:[/b] reality-altering energy waves that periodically sweep the zones
  - All loose food/plorts become "Unstable"
  - Plasma/flame energy erupts from ground cracks
[*][b]Unstable Items:[/b] jump out of your Vacpack on their own schedule
  - Pop out if you take damage
  - Vanish if left unstabilized
  - CANNOT use teleporters while carrying Unstable items
[*][b]Stabilization Methods:[/b]
  - Prisma Stabilizers (yellow portals, one-time use during disruptions)
  - Ancient Stabilizers (permanent archways, reusable)
[*][b]No Slime Sea:[/b] the bottom is cloud ocean. Anything that falls is PERMANENTLY LOST
[*][b]⚠️ Tarr Risk:[/b] very common here, thanks to high slime variety and abundant food
[/list]

[h3]Phase 2: Gordo Strategy & Elite Slime Acquisition[/h3]

[b]Pop These 3 Gordos:[/b]
[list]
[*][b]Twin Gordo[/b] (50 Fruit, Polaricherry counts 2x) = 12 Twin Slimes + Portal to Rainbow Fields
  - Location: Impossible Sky
[*][b]Sloomber Gordo[/b] (30 Meat, Candied Hen counts 2x) = 12 Sloomber Slimes + Portal to Conservatory
  - Location: Dream Land (North)
[*][b]Hyper Gordo[/b] (30 Veggies, Turbo Tater counts 2x) = 12 Hyper Slimes + Key switch
  - Location: Terrarium (requires Radiant Projector puzzle + 1 Hunter Plort + 1 Angler Plort)
[/list]

[b]Capture Strategy:[/b]
[list]
[*][b]Twin Slimes:[/b] 10-12 from Impossible Sky (pop the Gordo for 12 free)
[*][b]Sloomber Slimes:[/b] 10-12 from Dream Land (pop the Gordo for 12 free)
[*][b]Hyper Slimes:[/b] 10-12 from Terrarium ONLY (pop the Gordo for 12 free)
  - [b]⚠️ WARNING:[/b] Hyper Slimes are extremely bouncy and fast. Corral them last.
[*]Collect 40-60 of each favorite food (Polaricherry, Candied Hen, Turbo Tater)
[/list]

[h3]Phase 3: New Slime Species[/h3]

[b]Grey Labyrinth Exclusive Slimes:[/b]
[list]
[*][b]Shadow[/b]: cannot be vacuumed or captured
  - Produces plorts when hit by ANY thrown object (the item is not consumed)
  - Jumps when disturbed, then vanishes
  - Farm for Shadow Plorts (needed for doors + Chapter 14)
[*][b]Twin[/b] (Fruit, favorite: Polaricherry): alternates blue/pink states, opens portals to snag distant food
[*][b]Sloomber[/b] (Meat, favorite: Candied Hen): emits a sleep cloud that calms nearby slimes
[*][b]Hyper[/b] (Veggie, favorite: Turbo Tater): blistering fast, ricochets nonstop, demands maximum security
[/list]

[h3]Phase 4: Shadow & Prisma Plort Farming (Introduction)[/h3]

[b]Shadow Plort Farming:[/b]
[list]
[*]Hit Shadow Slimes with thrown objects (Pink Plorts, vegetables, anything to hand)
[*]They drop Shadow Plorts, then vanish
[*]Respawn in the same dark corners
[*][b](Patch 1.1.0) Shadow Pots:[/b] break open pots throughout the Grey Labyrinth to release Shadow Slimes. You can pick up and move pots for strategic farming.
[*][b](Patch 1.1.1) Despawn cap:[/b] Shadow Slimes now despawn once they hit their max plort count, so work multiple spawns instead of camping one. Pots also hold fewer slimes each.
[*]Total needed: ~845 Shadow Plorts (~825 for doors + 20 for Nullifier)
[*][b]⚠️ CRITICAL:[/b] store in SILOS, not the Refinery (Refinery plorts cannot open doors)
[/list]

[b]Prisma Plort Farming (Basic):[/b]
[list]
[*]During Prisma Disruptions, feed Unstable food to any slimes
[*]They produce Unstable Plorts
[*]Walk through an Ancient Stabilizer and Unstable Plorts convert to Prisma Plorts in inventory
[*]Conversion rate: 1 Unstable = 1-2 Prisma Plorts (typically 2)
[*]Total needed: ~245 Prisma Plorts (90 for Gigi trades + 155 for upgrades)
[*][b]⚠️ CRITICAL:[/b] store in SILOS, not the Refinery (Refinery plorts cannot be traded to Gigi)
[/list]
